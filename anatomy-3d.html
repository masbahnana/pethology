Skull

Function

Protects the brain and sense organs. Divided into cranium and facial bones. Jaw type varies by breed (dolichocephalic, mesocephalic, brachycephalic).

Clinical relevance

Brachycephalic breeds (Bulldogs, Pugs) often suffer from Brachycephalic Obstructive Airway Syndrome (BOAS).

Vertebral Column

Function

Provides structural support, protects the spinal cord, and allows flexible movement. Dogs have 7 cervical, 13 thoracic, 7 lumbar, 3 sacral vertebrae.

Clinical relevance

Intervertebral disc disease (IVDD) is especially common in Dachshunds and other chondrodystrophic breeds.

Ribcage

Function

Protects the heart and lungs. Dogs have 13 pairs of ribs — 9 true ribs attached to the sternum and 4 false ribs. Forms the thoracic cavity.

Clinical relevance

Rib fractures from trauma can cause pneumothorax. Palpation of the ribs is used to assess body condition score (BCS).

Pelvis

Function

Ring of bones (ilium, ischium, pubis) that supports the hindlimbs and protects pelvic organs. Forms the hip joint with the femur.

Clinical relevance

Pelvic fractures are common after road traffic accidents. Hip dysplasia affects the acetabulum and femoral head joint surface.

Femur

Function

Longest bone in the body. Forms the hip joint (ball-and-socket) proximally and the stifle joint (knee) distally with the tibia and patella.

Clinical relevance

Hip dysplasia and cranial cruciate ligament (CCL) rupture are among the most common orthopaedic conditions in dogs.

Scapula

Function

Triangular flat bone that forms the shoulder joint with the humerus. Dogs have no clavicle, so the scapula is held by muscles only, allowing greater stride length.

Clinical relevance

Osteochondrosis dissecans (OCD) of the shoulder joint affects the humeral head and is common in large, fast-growing breeds.
